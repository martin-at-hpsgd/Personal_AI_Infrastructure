# Interview Workflow — The Layering Interview

Take what the writer already has — a line, a folder of notes, a half-drafted chapter — and build the rest of the story WITH them, deriving the hidden structure their own material already implies.

## The Stance (load-bearing — this workflow augments a creator, never substitutes for one)

- Read the writer's material **before** asking anything. A generic questionnaire that ignores what they gave you is the failure this workflow exists to kill.
- **Never invent the spine — derive it from their seed.** Every element is a **proposal the writer ratifies**, its source labeled: **"inferred from your seed: <their words>"** vs. **"a new option I'm offering."** They must always be able to tell which is which.
- **Play every proposal back in the writer's own words** so they can correct it. A redirect is the truth, not a deviation — the writer's word always wins.
- **Options, not answers.** Offer two or three contrasting candidates to choose among; approving a plausible suggestion is not the same as creating it.
- **Deepen where the writer's energy and material already are.** Elicit from their pages before offering anything of your own; send genuinely-blank layers to **Explore**, never fill one silently.
- **Vibe/image-only seeds get elicitation, not derivation.** If they bring only a mood, pull texture and image out of them until there's enough of their own substrate to build on. Fabricating a flaw and plot from a mood, then asking them to "confirm" it, is substitution wearing a mask.
- **This workflow produces a spine, not prose.** Only WriteChapter emits story text.

## What "done" looks like

A saved `interview.md` at `LIFEOS/USER/CUSTOMIZATIONS/SKILLS/WriteStory/projects/<book-slug>/interview.md` containing:

- **Seed class** and the **derivation lens** used (one line each, with why)
- **The confirmed spine** — every element provenance-labeled (inferred-from-seed vs. offered) and every element ratified or redirected by the writer
- **Per-layer state** — each of the seven layers marked confirmed / partial / open
- **Favorites + reader target** — 3-5 stories the writer loves and what specifically they loved (voice + taste to anchor to); what they want a reader to feel on finishing (becomes a top-line Story Bible criterion)
- **A one-page rough spine** (Pressfield Foolscap: theme, dramatic question, hook, climax, three acts), marked explicitly disposable — a whole beginning-middle-end the writer can see early, with sane defaults offered rather than a meta-decision forced at every turn

This file is the resume point. If an existing project's `interview.md` / `ISA.md` are present, re-read and continue from current state — never restart.

## Seed classes

Classify what the writer brought — it sets how you proceed:

| Seed class | What they brought |
|---|---|
| **Character** | A person, a flaw, a voice |
| **Premise** | A "what if", a situation, a hook |
| **Plot** | A sequence of events, a structure |
| **Theme** | An argument, an idea, a question to explore |
| **World** | A setting, a system, a place |
| **Vibe / image** | A mood, a feeling, a fragment — no agent or causality yet |

## Derivation lenses

No single spine is correct. Pick the lens that fits the seed class, genre, and where the writer started; state which and why in one line. The lens sets only the **order** you reach the seven layers — they are always the destination. Full detail in `DerivationLenses.md`.

| Lens | Best for | Spine it derives |
|---|---|---|
| **Psychology-first** (Storr / Cron) | Character-arc, literary character studies | Sacred flaw / misbelief → dramatic question → plot as its test |
| **Structure-first** (Pressfield / Truby) | Genre, thriller, procedural, plot-driven | Hook-Build-Payoff / moral argument → character fitted to it |
| **Premise-first** (Egri) | Argument-driven, idea fiction | Cause→effect premise sentence → characters that prove it |
| **Theme-emergent** | Literary, experimental, slice-of-life | Hold theme loose; let it surface from scenes, derive it late |
| **Resonance-first** | Vibe / image / mood seeds | Texture and image before any causal spine |

**The psychology-first chain** — derive each element with the writer, as a labeled proposal, offering contrasting candidates when unsure:

1. **The false belief** — Storr's sacred flaw / Cron's misbelief, held to precision: not "she's insecure" but "she believes she's the only adult in any room."
2. **Origin damage** — the specific past moment that planted it.
3. **The dramatic question** — falls out of the flaw: *"will they stay [flaw] or become [not-flaw]?"* The engine of the book.
4. **Want vs. need** — want is what they chase; need is usually the inverse of the flaw.
5. **The opposition / pressure** — what forces the question. Not just a villain: a person, system, fate, society, time, institution, or the character's own contradiction — whichever actually presses on this flaw.
6. **Theme, in one sentence** — controlling idea (Pressfield) / premise (Egri) / moral argument (Truby); left open for theme-emergent lenses.

Other lenses derive the equivalent spine in their own order (see `DerivationLenses.md`). The move is always the same: *"You gave me X. That implies Y. Does that land?"* — provenance labeled.

## Handoff

Reflect the assembled spine back, name what's **confirmed** vs. still **open**, and let the writer choose the next step — they drive:

- **Deepen a thin layer** → **Explore**
- **Build the full structured plan** → **BuildBible** (turns the spine into the project ISA next to the interview)
- **Start writing the strongest scene now** → **WriteChapter**
